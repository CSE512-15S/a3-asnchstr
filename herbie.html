Ever wondered what the error of floating point programs looks like? The graph on the left shows you an overview of the error of the function sqrt(x+1) - sqrt(x), versus it's more accurate but mathematically equivilent version, 1/(sqrt(x+1) + sqrt(x)). But lines of average aren't the best way to see how the error is distributed. The graph on the right shows what portion of the points have what error. Move around the selection on the left graph to see what the error distribution is at different points in the floating point spectrum.
